About

Jurist, strategist, and trusted adviser to international institutions

Managing Partner, Scales Counsel — Partner, Alem & Associates (Beirut · Dubai · Abu Dhabi)

Rabih Maalouf is a senior legal practitioner and strategic adviser with over two decades of experience spanning the judiciary, international organizations, and high-stakes private practice. A former senior judge in Lebanon's commercial and criminal courts, he has adjudicated complex financial crime cases — including money laundering, corruption, fraud, tax evasion, and asset confiscation — and represented the Supreme Judicial Council before Parliament and public institutions in the reform of regulatory and legislative frameworks.

Following his judicial career, he has advised the International Monetary Fund (IMF), the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC), and the Geneva Centre for Security Sector Governance (DCAF) on AML/CFT compliance, financial integrity, mutual legal assistance, and institutional reform. He served as Special Counsel to the Abu Dhabi Judicial Department, establishing its Arbitration and Mediation Divisions, and as Senior Visiting Professional to the Special Tribunal for Lebanon at The Hague.

Beyond the law, Maalouf is an Artificial Intelligence Fellow at the Ford Foundation, a Visiting Lecturer in Constitutional Law and AI Policy at the American University of Beirut, and a CAMS-certified anti-money laundering specialist. He is an arbitrator at the London Court of International Arbitration (LCIA) and admitted to practice in Lebanon, New York, and Brazil.
