Areas of Focus

Four disciplines.
One strategic lens.

The most consequential problems rarely fit a single category. Maalouf's practice holds legal precision, institutional intelligence, and technology governance within a single, coherent advisory framework — informed by decades at the intersection of law and power.

01

AI & Technology Policy

As an AI Fellow at the Ford Foundation and lecturer in AI Policy at AUB, Maalouf advises governments and institutions on the governance of artificial intelligence — regulatory design, sovereign AI policy, liability frameworks, and the legal dimensions of machine intelligence. He engages decision-makers before the rules solidify.

02

Financial Crime & AML/CFT

CAMS-certified with deep judicial and advisory experience in anti-money laundering, counter-terrorism financing, sanctions regimes, asset recovery, and the cross-border mechanics of illicit finance. Former adviser to the IMF and UNODC on financial integrity frameworks and regulatory compliance. Active in legislative drafting and institutional reform.

03

Human Rights & International Law

Grounded in two decades on the bench and at the Special Tribunal for Lebanon, Maalouf advises on accountability mechanisms, transitional justice, extraterritorial proceedings, and the protection of fundamental rights under institutional or authoritarian pressure. His judicial rulings on individual freedoms garnered coverage by Human Rights Watch, NPR, and international media.

04

Strategic Power Advisory

Counsel to governments, boards, and senior principals on the architecture of influence — how regulatory frameworks are built and shaped, how institutions exercise and contest power, and how to navigate environments where law and politics are inseparable. Informed by experience advising the Abu Dhabi government, the Port of Beirut, and international multilateral bodies.
